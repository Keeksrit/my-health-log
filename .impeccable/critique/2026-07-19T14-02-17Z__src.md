## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Does it look AI-generated? Mostly no.** A genuine "Lab Notebook" identity — warm cream canvas, Lora + DM Sans, one forest-green accent, flat hairline cards, a hand-drawn anatomical body map, warm empty states. This is not template slop.

**LLM assessment — tells that DID fire:**
- **Tracked-uppercase eyebrow above every section (self-banned).** `.sec`/`.sectionLabel` (10px/600/0.07em/uppercase) headlines nearly every list section across Stats, Today, Calendar, Medications, Nutrition. DESIGN.md's own Don'ts ban exactly this. The single clearest template tell.
- **Emoji as the entire icon language** (📊📋📅💊🥗🧪🩹, ⎘✕⚠⚑📍) — renders inconsistently across platforms and reads consumer-casual, undercutting the "rigorous" half of the personality.
- Borderline: Stats opens with two 32px stat cards (mild flirt with the hero-metric pattern; not full-bleed).

**Deterministic scan (detector):** 114 findings — 113 advisory, 1 warning. Dominated by design-system **drift**, not slop: font-size off-ramp ×83 (13px ×33 is a de-facto undocumented body size), radius off-scale ×26 (10px ×14, 16px ×5), color off-token ×4. The **1 warning** is a genuine tell the review missed: **side-stripe** `border-left: 3px solid var(--accent)` at `src/pages/tests/ImportModal.module.css:22`.

**Where they disagree (important):** the detector's contrast and eyebrow rule families fired **zero** — yet the review found a serious AA contrast failure (ink3 ~2.2:1) and the eyebrow-on-every-section tell. The static detector can't resolve CSS-variable contrast or CSS-module uppercase patterns, so those are false negatives; trust the review there.

**Visual overlay:** unavailable. No browser-automation tool is exposed, and the app requires Supabase auth to render, so no live overlay/screenshot evidence exists — findings are source + detector only.

## Overall Impression

The bones are genuinely good: a disciplined single-source token system and a real point of view. What holds it back is not aesthetics (its strongest axis) but **honesty and accessibility under stress** — the app is beautiful on the happy path and quietly unsafe off it. The single biggest opportunity: make the app tell the truth when data fails to load, and make color-coded meaning survive color blindness and a clinician's black-and-white printout — the two things your own PRODUCT.md/DESIGN.md promise and the current build breaks.

## What's Working

1. **Disciplined single-source tokens.** `index.css` defines the palette + radii as vars; every module consumes them and the "One Green Rule" is genuinely honored. This is why it reads as designed, not generated.
2. **Redundant encoding where it was built deliberately.** `SevDots` encodes value by count filled AND color band; Nutrition filter-off uses opacity + line-through (not color); estimated training bands are diagonally striped vs solid. Thoughtful.
3. **Warm, guiding empty states.** Today / Nutrition / Medications pair icon + Lora heading + a next-step sentence. The "never boring" principle landing.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] Faint-ink tier fails WCAG AA.** `--ink3 #B2ADA4` ≈ 2.2:1 on white (worse on cream) yet carries timestamps, placeholders, inactive nav labels, axis ticks, hints. `--ink2 #6E6A62` drops to ~4.5:1 (borderline) on cream at body sizes. *Why:* DESIGN.md makes AA including muted/placeholder text non-negotiable. *Fix:* darken ink3 to ≈3:1 and use it for large/decorative only; push muted body text to ink2 or darker. *Command:* **colorize**.

**[P1] Data-load failures masquerade as empty states.** `App.tsx` and `Tests.tsx` catch fetch errors and set empty arrays; the user can't tell "no data" from "load failed" — a serene "Nothing logged yet" appears when the record may be unreachable. *Why:* catastrophic for a health record's trustworthiness; fails H9. *Fix:* explicit error surface with Retry, distinct from empty state; never show empty copy on a caught error. *Command:* **harden**.

**[P1] Nutrition timeline isn't touch- or color-blind-accessible.** Food-type identity rides only on 12px dot color; the name is in a hover tooltip that doesn't exist on touch; the dot is a 12px target where shift-click deletes; a 220px sidebar is forced into the 430px column. *Why:* violates the color-blind-safe principle AND the mobile register. *Fix:* redundant shape/label or a persistent legend; tap-toggle the tooltip; ≥44px hit areas; stack sidebar under the track on mobile. *Command:* **adapt** + **clarify**.

**[P2] Tracked-uppercase eyebrow on every section (self-banned) + inconsistent page titles.** `.sec` eyebrows headline almost every section while Tests uses a plain Lora h1. *Why:* it's DESIGN.md's banned pattern and reads templated; the title inconsistency breaks H4. *Fix:* sentence-case Lora/DM-Sans section titles; reserve tracked-uppercase for form field labels; one standard page-title component. Also fix the side-stripe at ImportModal.module.css:22. *Command:* **typeset**.

**[P3] No reduced-motion guard, no keyboard focus rings.** Zero `prefers-reduced-motion` blocks despite spin/slideUp/pulse keyframes; zero `:focus-visible` (only input border-color). *Why:* explicit accessibility requirements; keyboard users can't see focus on nav, body-map zones, chips, calendar cells. *Fix:* wrap animations in a reduced-motion guard; add a token-based `:focus-visible` outline globally. *Command:* **harden**.

## Persona Red Flags

**Sam (accessibility) — most exposed.** ink3 text at ~2.2:1; no `:focus-visible` anywhere; no reduced-motion guard; active nav conveyed by green color only (no weight/indicator); **Calendar day severity encoded only by pale tint** — no count/dot/icon, so a color-blind user or a B&W printout can't tell mild from severe. Directly breaks "encode meaning redundantly."

**Casey (mobile).** Nutrition forces a desktop flex-row + 220px sidebar into 430px; hover tooltip + 12px dot tap + shift-delete assume a mouse; Header hardcodes `padding-top:48px` instead of `env(safe-area-inset-top)` (BottomNav does honor safe-area — inconsistent); dupBtn/flagBtn ~16px tall, far below 44px.

**"The reviewing clinician" (your explicit second reader).** Opens cold: Calendar tints have no legend; Stats shows "avg severity 3.7" with no scale and no date range; timeline dot colors have no legend and no touch tooltip — can't decode the record without a walkthrough, defeating "legible to a second reader."

## Minor Observations

- Two loading idioms (spinner vs bare "Loading…"); product register prefers **skeletons** — none exist.
- `--danger` referenced in Nutrition is undefined in index.css (token is `--red`); relies on fallback.
- Symptom photos use `alt=""` — meaningful alt matters for the clinician/screen reader.
- CoverageBar falls back to off-palette `#eee`/`#888` when a key is missing.
- Sheet slideUp is only 50px with no scrim fade — subtler than DESIGN.md's "tactile" intent.
- Detector false positives to ignore: `#6AA84F` in a test file; 32/40px display numerals; rgba tint washes in Nutrition.

## Questions to Consider

1. If a clinician prints this in black & white, what survives? SevDots and striped training bands do; the Calendar severity map and every Nutrition dot **do not**. Is the Calendar actually compliant with your headline principle?
2. Why is the front-door action (Symptom) the 7th item in an overcrowded tab bar instead of a persistent FAB? Does a 7-tab bar respect "a tool held in one hand"?
3. Your riskiest moment — a failed load — renders as a serene "Nothing logged yet." Is calm the honest emotion there?
4. Emoji carry your entire icon language. Is 🩹/🧪 the "rigorous" signal you want a doctor to see?
